Culture & Heritage

Discover the deep cultural and historical roots of Northern Israel. From the mysticism of Safed to the ancient ruins of Caesarea Philippi, the north is rich in history, art, and tradition.

Safed - City of Kabbalah

Explore the mystical city of Safed, home to ancient synagogues, narrow alleys, and the heart of Jewish Kabbalah teachings. The artistic and spiritual atmosphere makes this city unique.

Caesarea Philippi

Visit the ancient ruins of Caesarea Philippi, one of the most significant archaeological sites in Northern Israel. Walk through the remains of the Roman city at the foot of Mount Hermon, where significant events from the Bible took place.

Druze Villages in the Golan Heights

Experience the unique culture and hospitality of the Druze community. Visit their villages, taste traditional food, and learn about their rich history in this part of the world.

Megiddo - The Biblical Armageddon

Visit the ancient city of Megiddo, known in biblical prophecy as the site of Armageddon. Explore the impressive ruins and learn about the strategic importance of this site in ancient history.

Museums and Art Galleries

Northern Israel is home to a variety of museums and art galleries, showcasing the rich history, religious significance, and artistic heritage of the region.

The Israel Museum - Safed

Located in Safed, this museum offers an immersive experience in Jewish art, spirituality, and history. It also hosts regular art exhibitions by local and international artists.

The Golan Heights Winery & Art Gallery

In addition to offering world-class wine tasting, this winery also hosts an art gallery with exhibitions dedicated to the natural beauty of the Golan Heights and the local history.

Beth Alpha Synagogue

Visit the ancient Beth Alpha Synagogue, one of the best-preserved ancient synagogues in Israel. Discover its intricate mosaics that depict biblical scenes and other historical images.
